Triggr

A Slyce product

You can't keep an eye on your devices 24/7, so Triggr alerts you when your device leaves a predefined area.

Triggr is currently in development. Sign up to get in line for early access and stay up to date.

Don't let your investment walk out the front door.

  • Location-based Security

    • Create custom location triggers that create a border your device is not supposed to cross.
    • Utilize cell towers as well as GPS when appropriate in order to optimize battery life.
    • Have the flexibility to determine how large or small the area your device can travel in without setting off a trigger.

  • Security Alerts

    • Receive an alert as soon as your devices pass the threshold you had set.
    • An SMS message will be sent to the phone(s) you designate so that you can be notified anytime and anywhere.

  • Power

    • Our location fencing allows you to create accurate regions while still conserving as much battery as possible.
    • If your device is supposed to be plugged in all the time, you can also have an alert sent to you if the device becomes unplugged from its station.

  • Customizable

    • Use our dedicated app or build the services into your own application with our SDK.
    • Choose the amount of phone numbers to get an SMS sent to.
    • Choose alerts based on a triggered area you create, whether your plugged in to power, or more.

  • Focus on your shot.

    iOS devices are popping up as tools on production sets, but they shouldn't be taking up your focus. Get notified immediately if one of the production iOS devices leaves the set.

  • Don't be afraid to leave your iPad at home.

    Set up a tight radius around your devices when they are home alone and plugged in. This ensures that you will be notified if someone gets a hold of your device and takes it somewhere else.

  • Make sure your business assets stay put.

    More iOS devices are being used as a Point Of Sale system, add some piece of mind by being able to be notified if your device is leaving the building.

  • These are only a few examples, what can you come up with?
